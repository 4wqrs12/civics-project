    What is pollution?

    It is the introduction of harmful materials, known as pollutants, into nature. These pollutants can originate from different sources, most common being natural and man-made. Some natural pollutants is from volcanoes, forest fires, and dust storms. Examples of man-made pollutants can come from gases of motor vehicles, waste from power plants, and soil pollution like pesticides.

    An example of natural pollution, which is the gas coming from the erupting volcano.

    An example of man-made pollution, which is the gas coming from the cars. This is a form of air pollution, since the pollutants from the cars are being released into the air.

    How does it affect us?

    Natural pollution does not harm us as much as man-made pollution, which can come in different forms and is constantly happening, making it dangerous to our planet. Air pollution, for example, can result in climate change, which is the change in global temperatures and weather patterns. Pollution can affect us too, as we can die earlier than we should, because of the health problems one would experience because of pollution. Some causes of these early deaths are from respiratory diseases, lung cancer, and other health issues caused by pollution.

    What are some simple things people can do to help decrease pollution?

    To help decrease pollution in your community, you should aim to buying local goods, since it would take less emmissions to transport it to its destination. You should reuse items such as plastic bags or bottles for later uses, or completely eliminate using it and go for reuseable bottles and bags. Another way you could help reduce pollution in your community is by using non-motor forms of transportation for places that are close to you. Some examples could be biking to your local supermarket or walking to your friend's house. Or if you need to use motor vehicles, you should aim for public transportation or carpooling whenever possible. There are still so many other ways you could help be part of the solution, but these are just to list a few.

    Recycling is a popular and easy way to help decrease plastic pollution in your community.

    What organizations can I join to further combat pollution?

    There is a possibilty that there is a local cleanup group in your community, which can focus on cleaning up public places like parks or beaches. You can also help these organizations by donating money to them, so you can still have a impact on the world without having to live a finger. For example, you could donate to The Ocean Cleanup, which is a nonprofit organization in the Netherlands that develops technology to remove plastic from oceans. But some local organizations, for example, in Massachusetts, is The Neponset River Watershed Association which aims to clean different parts of the Neponset River, and the surrounding lands too. There is also a organization which has partnered with many others, and it is earthday.org. This organization is more serious about their goals, and have have helped combat pollution in many different countries.

    The Earth Day organization will take many steps to make sure the use of plastic is reduced to combat pollution.
